# Japanese Business Terms Glossary

## Overview

This glossary provides definitions and context for Japanese business terms, cultural concepts, and terminology used throughout HankoSign Digital.

---

## Hanko & Seal Terms

### 判子 (はんこ / Hanko)
**Definition:** Traditional Japanese seal or stamp used for authentication on documents.

**Context:** In Japanese business culture, a hanko serves as a personal signature. Unlike Western signatures, hankos can be carved and reproduced, making their physical possession legally significant.

**Three Main Types:**
1. 認印 (Mitomein) - Acknowledgment seal
2. 銀行印 (Ginkoin) - Bank seal
3. 実印 (Jitsuin) - Registered seal

---

### 認印 (みとめいん / Mitomein)
**Definition:** Acknowledgment seal used for everyday documents.

**Characteristics:**
- No official registration required
- Used for receipts, memos, internal documents
- Lowest legal weight among the three seal types
- Can be purchased pre-made at stationery stores

**Use Cases:**
- Package delivery receipts
- Attendance sheets
- Meeting minutes
- Casual acknowledgments

---

### 銀行印 (ぎんこういん / Ginkoin)
**Definition:** Bank seal registered with financial institutions.

**Characteristics:**
- Registered with banks for account transactions
- Used for bank withdrawals, transfers, loan applications
- Should be kept separate from daily-use seals
- Medium legal weight

**Security Notes:**
- Never use the same seal for bank and daily use
- Store securely like a PIN code
- Changing requires visiting bank branch

---

### 実印 (じついん / Jitsuin)
**Definition:** Officially registered personal seal with government authority.

**Characteristics:**
- Registered at city/ward office (市区町村)
- Highest legal authority among seals
- Required for major contracts and legal documents
- Comes with 印鑑証明書 (inkan shōmeisho) certificate

**Required For:**
- Real estate transactions
- Vehicle registration
- Establishing a company
- Marriage registration
- Inheritance documents
- Loan guarantees

**Registration Process:**
1. Create unique seal (kanji, often surname only)
2. Register at local municipality office
3. Receive registration card
4. Obtain certificates (印鑑証明書) when needed

---

### 印鑑証明書 (いんかんしょうめいしょ / Inkan Shōmeisho)
**Definition:** Certificate proving the authenticity and registration of a jitsuin.

**Purpose:** Verifies that a seal impression matches the officially registered jitsuin.

**Validity:** Usually 3 months from issue date

**Cost:** ¥300-500 per certificate

---

### 朱肉 (しゅにく / Shuniku)
**Definition:** Red ink paste used with Japanese seals.

**Color:** Traditional red/vermillion (#D32F2F in digital)

**Symbolism:** Red has been used since ancient China to represent authority and authenticity.

---

### 篆書体 (てんしょたい / Tenshotai)
**Definition:** Seal script, an ancient Chinese calligraphy style used for seals.

**Characteristics:**
- Highly decorative and difficult to forge
- Traditional choice for jitsuin
- Hard to read for non-experts (adds security)

**Other Seal Fonts:**
- 古印体 (Kointai) - Old seal script
- 隷書体 (Reishotai) - Clerical script

---

## Business Workflow Terms

### 稟議 (りんぎ / Ringi)
**Definition:** Japanese decision-making process involving circulation and approval of proposals.

**Process:**
1. Proposer creates 稟議書 (ringi-sho / proposal document)
2. Document circulates to relevant managers/departments
3. Each party applies hanko for approval
4. Reaches final decision-maker for approval
5. Implemented if approved

**Cultural Significance:**
- Reflects consensus-based decision making (nemawashi)
- Spreads responsibility across organization
- Can be slow but ensures buy-in
- Documents decision trail

---

### 稟議書 (りんぎしょ / Ringi-sho)
**Definition:** Proposal document used in the ringi process.

**Typical Contents:**
- Proposal title and summary
- Background and rationale
- Budget requirements
- Expected benefits
- Risk analysis
- Approval signature blocks

---

### 根回し (ねまわし / Nemawashi)
**Definition:** Informal consensus building before formal decisions.

**Literal Meaning:** "Going around the roots" (gardening term)

**Practice:** Meeting with stakeholders individually before formal meetings to build support and address concerns.

**Result:** By the time a formal decision meeting occurs, opposition is minimized and consensus is likely.

---

### 承認 (しょうにん / Shōnin)
**Definition:** Approval or authorization.

**In HankoSign Digital:** The act of applying one's hanko to approve a document or proposal.

---

### 代理 (だいり / Dairi)
**Definition:** Proxy or representative acting on behalf of another.

**Use Case:** Manager A authorizes Manager B to apply hanko on their behalf when absent.

**Requirements:**
- Written authorization
- Clear scope of authority
- Record of proxy arrangement

---

## Document Terms

### 契約書 (けいやくしょ / Keiyaku-sho)
**Definition:** Contract or agreement document.

**Legal Requirements:**
- Clear terms and conditions
- Signatures/seals from all parties
- Date of agreement
- Validity period (if applicable)

**Common Types:**
- 雇用契約書 (Employment contract)
- 業務委託契約書 (Service agreement)
- 売買契約書 (Sales contract)
- 秘密保持契約書 (NDA)

---

### 請求書 (せいきゅうしょ / Seikyu-sho)
**Definition:** Invoice or bill.

**Required Elements:**
- Invoice number
- Issue date
- Payment due date
- Itemized charges
- Seller's hanko (optional but common)

---

### 見積書 (みつもりしょ / Mitsumori-sho)
**Definition:** Quotation or estimate.

**Purpose:** Preliminary pricing before formal contract.

**Validity:** Usually 30-60 days

---

### 領収書 (りょうしゅうしょ / Ryōshū-sho)
**Definition:** Receipt for payment.

**Tax Purposes:** Required for expense reports and tax deductions.

**Legal Requirement:** Must include hanko for amounts over ¥30,000.

---

### 発注書 (はっちゅうしょ / Hacchū-sho)
**Definition:** Purchase order.

**Function:** Official request to supplier to provide goods/services.

---

### 報告書 (ほうこくしょ / Hōkoku-sho)
**Definition:** Report document.

**Types:**
- 進捗報告書 (Progress report)
- 業務報告書 (Business report)
- 調査報告書 (Investigation report)

---

## Legal & Compliance Terms

### 電子署名法 (でんししょめいほう / Denshi Shomei Hō)
**Definition:** Electronic Signature Act (Law No. 102 of 2000).

**Purpose:** Establishes legal framework for electronic signatures in Japan.

**Key Provisions:**
- Electronic signatures have same legal effect as handwritten signatures
- Requirements for certified electronic signatures
- Role of certification providers

**Website:** [https://www.digital.go.jp/](https://www.digital.go.jp/)

---

### 電子帳簿保存法 (でんしちょうぼほぞんほう / Denshi Chōbo Hozon Hō)
**Definition:** Electronic Books Preservation Act.

**Purpose:** Regulates storage of accounting documents in electronic format.

**Requirements:**
- Timestamp preservation
- Searchability
- Tamper-prevention
- Audit trail

**Revision (2022):** Relaxed requirements for electronic storage, making digitalization easier.

---

### 個人情報保護法 (こじんじょうほうほごほう / Kojin Jōhō Hogo Hō)
**Definition:** Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI).

**Equivalent:** Similar to GDPR in Europe.

**Requirements:**
- User consent for data collection
- Secure storage
- Right to access and deletion
- Notification of data breaches

---

### 法人番号 (ほうじんばんごう / Hōjin Bangō)
**Definition:** Corporate Number (13-digit identifier for businesses).

**Purpose:** Unique identifier for legal entities in Japan.

**Format:** 1234567890123 (13 digits)

**Assignment:** Automatically assigned when company is registered.

**Public Information:** Searchable at [https://www.houjin-bangou.nta.go.jp/](https://www.houjin-bangou.nta.go.jp/)

---

### マイナンバー (My Number)
**Definition:** Individual Number (12-digit social security and tax number).

**Purpose:** Unified identification for tax, social security, disaster response.

**Format:** 123456789012 (12 digits)

**Privacy:** Highly protected personal information with strict handling requirements.

---

## Business Culture Terms

### 敬語 (けいご / Keigo)
**Definition:** Honorific language used to show respect in Japanese.

**Types:**
- 尊敬語 (Sonkeigo) - Respectful language
- 謙譲語 (Kenjōgo) - Humble language
- 丁寧語 (Teineigo) - Polite language

**Importance:** Essential in business software interfaces, especially for approval workflows.

---

### 上司 (じょうし / Jōshi)
**Definition:** Superior or boss.

**Hierarchy Impact:** Approval workflows typically flow from subordinate to superior.

---

### 部 (ぶ / Bu) / 課 (か / Ka)
**Definition:** Department (Bu) and Section (Ka).

**Organizational Structure:**
```
会社 (Kaisha - Company)
  ↓
部 (Bu - Department)
  ↓
課 (Ka - Section)
  ↓
係 (Kakari - Subsection)
```

**Example:**
- 営業部 (Eigyō-bu) - Sales Department
- 総務部 (Sōmu-bu) - General Affairs Department
- 経理課 (Keiri-ka) - Accounting Section

---

### 役職 (やくしょく / Yakushoku)
**Definition:** Job title or position.

**Common Titles:**
- 社長 (Shachō) - President/CEO
- 専務 (Senmu) - Senior Managing Director
- 部長 (Buchō) - Department Manager
- 課長 (Kachō) - Section Manager
- 係長 (Kakarichō) - Subsection Chief
- 主任 (Shunin) - Chief clerk
- 平社員 (Hira-shain) - Regular employee

---

## Technology Terms

### デジタル庁 (Digital Chō)
**Definition:** Digital Agency of Japan (established September 2021).

**Mission:** Promote digital transformation of government and society.

**Key Initiatives:**
- My Number Card promotion
- Elimination of fax and hanko from government
- Cloud adoption
- Interoperability standards

**Website:** [https://www.digital.go.jp/](https://www.digital.go.jp/)

---

### 2025年の崖 (2025-nen no Gake)
**Definition:** "The 2025 Digital Cliff" - term coined by Japan's METI.

**Warning:** Economic losses up to ¥12 trillion annually if legacy systems aren't modernized.

**Context:** HankoSign Digital addresses this by digitalizing the hanko system.

---

### DX (Digital Transformation - デジタルトランスフォーメーション)
**Definition:** Integration of digital technology into business operations.

**Japan Context:** Major national priority with government incentives and support programs.

---

## Related Terms

### FAX (ファックス)
**Status:** Still widely used in Japan (84% of companies in 2024).

**Cultural Reason:** Trusted document transmission method, paper trail, perceived security.

**DX Challenge:** Major target for digitalization efforts.

---

### 紙文化 (かみぶんか / Kami Bunka)
**Definition:** "Paper culture" - preference for physical documents.

**Challenge for DX:** Resistance to fully digital workflows due to comfort with physical processes.

---

## Pronunciation Guide

### Romanization System
This glossary uses the Hepburn romanization system, the most common for Japanese.

### Vowels
- **a**: ah (like "father")
- **i**: ee (like "eat")
- **u**: oo (like "food")
- **e**: eh (like "get")
- **o**: oh (like "go")

### Long Vowels
- **ō**: Extended "o" sound (held longer)
- **ū**: Extended "u" sound
